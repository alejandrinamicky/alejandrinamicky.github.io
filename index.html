About

Alejandrina Guzman

Alejandrina is a fourth year studying government and Mexican American Studies in the Liberal Arts Honors program from the small town of Azle, Texas. When she’s not zooming around campus, she dedicates her time in the Multicultural Engagement Center (MEC) as an agency director in Latino Community Affairs (LCA), and is also an active member in Orange Jackets and League of United Latin American Citizens.

Micky Wolf

Micky is a junior from San Antonio studying Plan II and Business Honors as a part of the Forty Acres Scholarship Program. He is currently the Student Affairs Committee Chair and a University-Wide Representative within SG. He also is in ZBT fraternity and runs 2 organizations he co-founded: Texas for Expanding Opportunity, an after-school extracurricular mentorship program, and Texas Leaders, an organization that puts on leadership events and trainings for UT’s top student leaders.

INSERT COMBINED WHY THEY'RE RUNNING HERE

Her first two years in college, she didn’t like SG because she thought it was an exclusive organization that accomplished little. In February of her sophomore year, her friend approached her to talk about making SG a more inclusive space. While Alejandrina was initially pumped, she became very hesitant when she realized the friend wanted her to run. She ended up turning in her forms an hour before the deadline, and after serving as a University-Wide Representative last year, she realized that SG is a vehicle for improving campus. She has seen how especially important representation is in order to advocate for underrepresented communities on campus. Her different identities - being differently abled, Latina, and a woman - allows her to bring together different communities to the frontlines of spaces that they may not have otherwise been involved with. For her, running for this position is all about representation, advocacy, and empowerment.

Micky is a 3rd generation Longhorn who used to cry when UT would lose football games. While he no longer cries after losses, at least publicly, his passion for UT has only grown. He finds he is most happy when feeling connected to something greater than himself or seeing the tangible impact his work has on others. When a friend told him that the Texas Leaders Conference was the best 5 hours he’d ever spent at UT, Micky realized he wanted to have that type of impact on student’ experiences as much as possible. While his interests and involvement at UT so far have been quite varied, he thinks that executive alliance will enable him to create the most impact on other students. He feels that he will never love another place as much as he loves the Forty Acres, and, for him, doing this role is all about serving and giving back to this university as much as possible.
